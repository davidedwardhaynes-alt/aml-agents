## Thailand — STR Filing Guidance

### Legal basis
- **AMLA** — Anti-Money Laundering Act B.E. 2542 (1999), as amended (major amendments 2003, 2008, 2009, 2013, 2015, 2019)
- **CTPF Act** — Counter-Terrorism and Proliferation of Weapons of Mass Destruction Financing Financing Act B.E. 2559 (2016)
- Ministerial Regulations issued under AMLA — designate the predicate offences, reporting institutions, thresholds, forms and timing
- Sectoral notices: **BOT** (banks), **SEC** (capital markets, digital asset operators), **OIC** (insurers)

### Who must file
- Banks and finance companies — BOT-supervised
- Securities companies, derivatives intermediaries, mutual fund managers, custodians — SEC-supervised
- Digital asset operators (exchanges, brokers, dealers, fund managers, custodians) — SEC-registered
- Life insurance and non-life insurance companies — OIC-supervised
- Money changers, remittance providers (other than commercial banks)
- Asset management companies, financial-business operators registered under the AMLA
- DNFBPs designated by Ministerial Regulation No. 11 (B.E. 2562 / 2019):
  - Lawyers and notarial-services practitioners (captured activities only)
  - Accountants (captured activities only)
  - Real estate agents and developers
  - Dealers in precious metals, precious stones, jewellery
  - Antique dealers
  - Personal loan / car-title-loan / pawnbroker operators above threshold

### Threshold for filing
"Has reasonable grounds to believe" that a transaction may be:
- related to the proceeds of a predicate offence under AMLA Section 3, or
- related to terrorism financing or proliferation financing (CTPF Act)
- structured to evade reporting

…then **STR** must be filed under **AMLA Section 13**.

Separate **CTR / cash transaction reports** are also mandatory (without suspicion) when transactions cross the threshold:
- Cash transactions ≥ **THB 2,000,000**
- Property transactions ≥ **THB 5,000,000**
- Cross-border physical movement of cash ≥ **USD 20,000** equivalent (declared at customs)

> **Reasonable grounds** is an objective standard. The bar is suspicion, not proof.

### Timing
- File the STR within **7 working days** of the date on which the reporting institution forms suspicion (AMLA Section 13)
- Industry practice for major banks: within **3 business days** of MLRO escalation
- **Terrorism financing** under the CTPF Act: file without delay; assets associated with a designated person must be frozen immediately on designation

### Filing channel
**AMLO E-Reporting System (ERS)** — operated by the Anti-Money Laundering Office (สำนักงานป้องกันและปราบปรามการฟอกเงิน, สปง.).

- Web-based portal at https://ers.amlo.go.th
- Reporting institution's MLRO (or designated officer) holds the account
- Bulk-upload (XML) supported for CTRs at large institutions
- Paper filing accepted only by exception (system outage, sectors not yet onboarded)

### Required content
1. Identity of the subject and connected parties (incl. ultimate beneficial owners)
2. Account / relationship details — open date, product, branch
3. Description of the suspicious activity — transactions, dates, amounts in THB and original currency, channels, counterparties
4. Indicia of suspicion (red flags observed) — mapped to AMLO typology bulletins where possible
5. Reasonable grounds for suspicion — the analyst's narrative
6. Action taken — account status, internal escalation, customer-facing actions (carefully)
7. Supporting documents (account statements, KYC files, customer correspondence)

### Tipping-off (AMLA Section 25 and 65)
**Strict prohibition.** Do NOT disclose to the customer (or any third party) that:
- An STR has been or is being filed
- The matter has been referred to AMLO or other law enforcement
- An investigation is in progress

The customer may be told that an account is restricted or closed for "regulatory compliance reasons" only. No specifics.

**Penalty:** imprisonment up to **3 years** and/or fine up to **THB 300,000**.

### Record retention
**Minimum 5 years** from the close of the relationship or the date of the transaction (whichever is later) — STR itself, supporting documents, internal escalations, customer communications, decision rationale (AMLA Section 22).

### Penalties (non-compliance)
- Failure to file STR — fine up to **THB 500,000** + continuing penalty of **THB 5,000 per day** of default
- Failure to file CTR — fine up to **THB 300,000** + continuing penalty
- Failure to conduct CDD — fine up to **THB 500,000**
- Tipping-off — up to **3 years** imprisonment and/or fine up to **THB 300,000**
- Corporate directors / officers may be held personally liable where the institution's failure is attributable to their acquiescence (AMLA Section 62)

## Japan — STR Filing Guidance

### Legal basis
- **APTCP Act** — Act on Prevention of Transfer of Criminal Proceeds (犯罪による収益の移転防止に関する法律)
- **Cabinet Order on Prevention of Transfer of Criminal Proceeds**
- **Ordinance for Enforcement of the Act on Prevention of Transfer of Criminal Proceeds**
- **Sotaihō** — Act on Punishment of Organized Crimes, Control of Proceeds of Crime and Other Matters (組織的犯罪処罰法)
- **Mayaku Tokurei-hō** — Anti-Drug Special Provisions Act (麻薬特例法)
- **Public Intimidation Crimes Financing Act** — terrorism-financing offences
- Sectoral rules: **FSA AML/CFT Inspection Manual** (banks, FIs); **FSA Crypto-Asset Exchange Service Provider AML/CFT Guidelines**; **METI Guidelines for the Real Estate Sector**; industry-association AML guidelines for legal, accounting, judicial-scrivener professions

### Who must file
**FSA-supervised** financial institutions:
- Banks (city banks, regional banks, *shinkin* banks, *shinyo kumiai* credit cooperatives)
- Securities firms (Type I FIBOs)
- Investment management / advisory firms (Type II FIBOs)
- Life insurers, non-life insurers
- Money lenders (consumer finance, SME finance)
- Crypto-asset exchange service providers (*Anshō Kōkan-gyōsha* — registered under the Payment Services Act, e.g. bitFlyer, Coincheck, BitTrade)
- Funds-transfer service providers (*Shikin Idō-gyōsha*)
- Prepaid payment-instrument issuers (above threshold)

**METI-supervised:** real estate agents, dealers in precious metals and stones (*kikinzoku-shōnin*).

**Other Tokutei Jigyōsha (specified business operators)** under APTCP Act Article 2:
- Attorneys (*bengoshi*) — when conducting designated transactions on behalf of clients
- Judicial scriveners (*shihō shoshi*)
- Certified public accountants (*kōnin kaikei-shi*)
- Tax accountants (*zeirishi*)
- Trust and corporate-service providers

### Threshold for filing
"Suspicion" that the transaction relates to criminal proceeds (APTCP Act Article 8(1)). Specifically:
- The transaction has no clear economic justification
- The customer cannot adequately explain the source of funds
- The transaction pattern deviates materially from the customer's profile
- The transaction is structured to avoid being captured by CDD or reporting thresholds
- The transaction involves a counterparty connected to organised crime, sanctions, or politically exposed person concerns

### Timing
- File **without delay** (*ichihaku-naku*) once suspicion is formed (APTCP Act Article 8(1)).
- Industry practice for FSA-supervised firms targets median filing within 30 days of internal escalation; same-day for high-risk patterns.
- For terrorism financing under the Public Intimidation Crimes Financing Act, file **immediately**.
- Cross-border wire transfers above JPY 30 million trigger a parallel **Foreign Exchange Act (FEFTA)** notification to MOF/Customs (separate from the STR).

### Filing channel
**JAFIC** (Japan Financial Intelligence Center) — within the Criminal Investigation Bureau of the National Police Agency. STRs are submitted through the JAFIC online portal using the prescribed XML schema. Designated AML compliance officers (*Hōkoku Sekinin-sha*) hold the digital credentials.

### Required content
1. *Tokutei Jigyōsha* identification — name, supervisor, sector classification
2. Customer (natural or juridical) — identity verification record (*Honnin Tokutei Jikō Kakunin Kiroku*) and beneficial owner (*Jikkitsu Shihai-sha*)
3. Transaction(s) giving rise to suspicion — date, channel, amount, counterparty
4. Reasons for suspicion — narrative referencing the relevant Sotaihō / Mayaku tokurei-hō predicate offence(s) and any matching JAFIC reference cases
5. Action taken by the reporting institution

### Predicate offences (Sotaihō and related — abbreviated)
- Organised-crime offences (the *boryokudan* / yakuza control framework)
- Drug trafficking (under Mayaku tokurei-hō)
- Specialised fraud (*tokushu sagi*) — including "It's me / *ore-ore*" scams, refund-fraud, and BCM variants
- Voice phishing and SMS-phishing scams
- Illegal foreign-exchange dealing
- Illegal gambling
- Tax evasion (where threshold met)
- Bribery and political-funding offences
- Trafficking in persons
- Counterfeiting and intellectual-property crimes
- Cybercrime and unauthorised computer access offences
- Terrorism financing (under the Public Intimidation Crimes Financing Act)

### Tipping-off (告知禁止 *kokuchi kinshi*)
APTCP Act Article 8(3) prohibits the *Tokutei Jigyōsha* and its officers, employees, and agents from disclosing the fact of an STR to the customer or to any unauthorised party. Customer communications about transaction holds, EDD inquiries, or account closures must be drafted to comply.

### Penalties for non-filing
APTCP Act Articles 25-26 prescribe administrative fines up to JPY 1 million and criminal penalties (imprisonment up to 6 months and/or fines up to JPY 500,000) for officers found responsible for non-filing or for tipping-off. Repeat offences and corporate fines can scale higher.

### Practical tips
- JAFIC publishes annual *Sankō Jirei* (reference cases) — incorporate the case-number where the situation matches a published typology. The *Tokushu Sagi* mule-victim typology is the most-cited in retail-banking STRs.
- For Crypto-Asset Exchange Service Provider STRs, cross-reference the FSA Crypto-Asset Exchange Service Provider AML/CFT Guidelines and the JVCEA self-regulation rules.
- For real estate cases, the METI guidelines provide red-flag indicators specific to that sector — e.g., cash deposits by overseas-resident buyers without source-of-funds documentation.
- North-Korea sanctions: any DPRK nexus triggers parallel notification to METI under FEFTA's North Korea sanctions framework (independent of the STR obligation).

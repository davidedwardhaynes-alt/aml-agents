## Malaysia — STR Filing Guidance

### Legal basis
- **AMLA** (Anti-Money Laundering, Anti-Terrorism Financing and Proceeds of Unlawful Activities Act 2001)
- **AMLA s.14** — STR filing obligation
- **AMLA s.79** — tipping-off prohibition
- BNM AML/CFT Sectoral Guidelines (Banking, Insurance, Capital Market Intermediaries, MSB, DNFBPs, e-money, DAEs)

### Who must file
Reporting institutions per AMLA First Schedule:
- Licensed banks, Islamic banks, investment banks, development financial institutions
- Insurance and takaful operators
- Capital market intermediaries (SC-licensed)
- Money services businesses (remittance, money-changing, wholesale currency)
- E-money issuers and payment system operators
- Digital asset exchanges (DAEs) — SC-registered
- DNFBPs — lawyers, accountants, company secretaries, trust companies, real estate agents, casinos and gaming operators (Genting), precious stones/metals dealers, registered estate agents

### Threshold for filing
"Knows or **has reason to suspect**" that any transaction or proposed transaction may:
- involve proceeds of an unlawful activity (AMLA Second Schedule offences), or
- relate to terrorism financing

> **"Reason to suspect"** is a lower bar than knowledge. Includes attempted transactions, regardless of amount.

### Timing
- "Promptly" — BNM guidance specifies **within next working day** of suspicion forming, though earlier filing is encouraged
- Suspicious activity that hits any **threshold-based reporting** trigger (CTR over RM 25,000) requires separate threshold reporting

### Filing channel
**FINS** — Financial Intelligence Network System, operated by FIED (Financial Intelligence and Enforcement Department) of Bank Negara Malaysia.

Filing is electronic via FINS Online. Reporting institutions must register with FIED to obtain credentials.

### Required content
1. Subject details — name, ID number (NRIC / passport), date of birth, address
2. Account / relationship information
3. Beneficial ownership (where applicable)
4. Description of suspicious transaction(s) — date, amount, currency, mode (cash, wire, cheque, e-transfer)
5. Originator and beneficiary information
6. Grounds for suspicion (the narrative)
7. Action taken by the reporting institution
8. Supporting documents

### Tipping-off (AMLA s.79)
**Strict prohibition.** Do not disclose to the customer or any third party that:
- An STR has been filed or is being filed
- An investigation is underway
- Information has been provided to authorities

**Penalty:** up to **RM 1,000,000** fine and/or **1 year** imprisonment.

### Record retention
**Minimum 6 years** from the date of completion of the transaction or termination of the business relationship — whichever is later. Includes STR itself, CDD records, transaction records, MLRO decision file.

### Penalties (non-compliance)
- Failure to file STR (s.14): up to **RM 1,000,000** fine, 1 year imprisonment, or both
- Tipping-off (s.79): up to **RM 1,000,000** fine, 1 year imprisonment, or both
- Failure to maintain records (s.17): up to **RM 1,000,000** fine
- BNM administrative actions and enforcement separate from criminal liability

### Special considerations
- **Threshold reporting** — Cash Transaction Reports (CTR) for transactions ≥ RM 25,000 are separate from STRs. CTR is automatic; STR is judgment-based on suspicion.
- **Sanctions screening** — separate AMLA s.66B obligations for UN/MOHA-listed entities. Sanctions hits typically also generate STRs.
- **Halal-compliant institutions** — Islamic banks under same AMLA regime.

## Hong Kong — STR Filing Guidance

### Legal basis
- **OSCO** (Organized and Serious Crimes Ordinance, Cap. 455) — s.25A
- **DTROP** (Drug Trafficking (Recovery of Proceeds) Ordinance, Cap. 405) — s.25A
- **UNATMO** (United Nations (Anti-Terrorism Measures) Ordinance, Cap. 575) — s.12
- **AMLO** (Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorist Financing Ordinance, Cap. 615) — sectoral CDD/RM obligations
- Sector guidelines: HKMA, SFC, IA, C&ED Guidelines on AML/CFT

### Who must file
- Authorized institutions — banks (HKMA-regulated)
- Licensed corporations (SFC) — securities and futures dealers, asset managers
- Authorized insurers and licensed insurance intermediaries (IA-regulated)
- Money service operators — remitters, money changers (C&ED-licensed)
- Trust or company service providers (TCSPs)
- DNFBPs — solicitors, accountants, real estate agents, precious metals/stones dealers
- Stored value facility (SVF) licensees
- Virtual asset service providers (VASPs) — under the new licensing regime (SFC)

### Threshold for filing
"Knows or **suspects**" that any property in whole or in part directly or indirectly represents:
- proceeds of an indictable offence (OSCO s.25A)
- proceeds of drug trafficking (DTROP s.25A)
- terrorist property (UNATMO s.12)

> The threshold is **suspicion** — lower than belief or knowledge. No requirement to verify before filing; report and let JFIU determine.

### Timing
- "As soon as it is reasonably practicable" after the suspicion is formed
- Industry practice: within **5 business days** of MLRO escalation
- **Terrorism financing: file without delay** (UNATMO)

### Filing channel
**STREAMS** — Suspicious Transaction Report Electronic Auto-Reporting System, operated by JFIU (Joint Financial Intelligence Unit, run jointly by HKPF and Customs & Excise).

### Required content
1. Identity of subject (customer, account holder, beneficial owner)
2. Account / relationship details
3. Suspicious transactions — dates, amounts, currencies, counterparties, channels
4. Reason for suspicion (the narrative)
5. Action taken
6. Supporting documents (bank statements, KYC, customer correspondence)

### Tipping-off
**Strict prohibition** under OSCO s.25A(5), DTROP s.25A(5), UNATMO s.14.

You may not disclose to the subject — or anyone other than for the purposes of the disclosure — that an STR has been made or is being made.

**Penalty:** up to **HK$500,000** fine and **3 years** imprisonment.

### Consent regime (a HK-specific feature)
Under OSCO s.25A(2)(a), if you receive consent from JFIU to deal with reported property, you have a defence against the s.25 offence (dealing with proceeds of indictable offence). In practice:
- File the STR
- Where possible, **do not act on the suspect funds until JFIU responds** (typically 7 working days)
- A "no consent" letter from JFIU effectively freezes the funds

### Record retention
**Minimum 5 years** under AMLO Sch. 2 — STR records, supporting documents, MLRO decision file.

### Penalties (non-compliance)
- Failure to disclose (s.25A(7)): up to **HK$50,000** fine, 3 months imprisonment (individual)
- Tipping-off: up to **HK$500,000** fine, 3 years imprisonment
- Dealing with proceeds without filing (s.25): up to **HK$5 million** fine, 14 years imprisonment

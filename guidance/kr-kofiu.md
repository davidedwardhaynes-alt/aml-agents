## Korea — STR Filing Guidance

### Legal basis
- **FTRA** — Act on Reporting and Use of Specific Financial Transaction Information (특정 금융거래정보의 보고 및 이용에 관한 법률)
- **FTRA Enforcement Decree** (시행령)
- **Act on Regulation and Punishment of Concealment of Criminal Proceeds** (범죄수익은닉의 규제 및 처벌 등에 관한 법률) — predicate offence anchor
- **Anti-Corruption Act** (부패방지권익위원회법)
- **Foreign Exchange Transactions Act** (외국환거래법) — for cross-border flows
- Sectoral rules: **FSC Notice** on AML/CFT for financial institutions; **KoFIU Notice** for VASPs (FTRA Article 5-2 supplementary guidance, 2021 onward); **FSS Inspection Manual** for AML/CFT inspections

### Who must file
**FSC / FSS-supervised financial institutions** (the primary FTRA *Bogo Gigwan*):
- Commercial banks (시중은행 — KB, Shinhan, Hana, Woori, NH; foreign-bank branches)
- Special banks (특수은행 — IBK, KEB Hana, KDB)
- Mutual savings banks (저축은행)
- Securities firms (증권회사)
- Asset management companies (자산운용회사) and investment trusts
- Insurers (생명보험 / 손해보험)
- Credit-card companies and capital companies (여신전문금융회사)
- E-money issuers and electronic-payment-service providers (전자지급결제대행업, 결제대행업)

**KoFIU-direct (added in 2021 amendments):**
- Virtual asset service providers (가상자산사업자 — VASPs) — Upbit, Bithumb, Coinone, Korbit and any FTRA-registered domestic or foreign-registered exchange operating in Korea

**Other:**
- Casinos (Kangwon Land, Paradise, Grand Korea Leisure)
- Notaries and lawyers (한정적 — only when conducting specified financial transactions)

### Threshold for filing
"Reasonable suspicion" (FTRA Article 4(1)) that:
- The transaction relates to criminal proceeds under the Concealment of Criminal Proceeds Act, or
- The customer's identity may be false, or
- The transaction may be related to terrorism financing.

The threshold is objective — no certainty required. Internal escalation criteria (specific amounts, counterparty types, deviation from profile) are set by each *Bogo Gigwan*'s AML programme.

### Timing
- File **without delay** (*jichae-eobsi*) — for routine STRs, within 30 days; for terrorism-financing-suspected transactions, immediately.
- CTR (Currency Transaction Report) for cash transactions ≥ **KRW 10 million** in a single day is filed automatically and separately. STR is filed in addition where suspicion exists.

### Filing channel
**KoFIU** — within the Financial Services Commission. STRs are submitted through the KoFIU electronic reporting system using the prescribed XML schema. Designated Compliance Officers (*Junseo Tamdang Imja*) hold the digital credentials.

### Required content
1. *Bogo Gigwan* identification — name, FSC licence number, sector classification
2. Customer (*Goegaek*) — full identity, RRN (*Jumin Deungrok Beonho*) for natural persons or BRN (*Saeop-ja Deungrok Beonho*) for juridical persons, beneficial owner (*Silsoyuja*)
3. Transaction(s) giving rise to suspicion — date, channel, amount, counterparty
4. Reasons for suspicion — narrative referencing the relevant Concealment of Criminal Proceeds Act predicate offence(s)
5. Action taken by the reporting institution

### Predicate offences (Concealment of Criminal Proceeds Act schedule — abbreviated)
- Voice phishing (*boishu pishing*) and other electronic financial fraud
- Illegal pyramid / multi-level marketing schemes (*bul-beob piramideu*)
- Boram-gye and rotating-credit scam variants
- Illegal foreign-exchange dealing (*bul-beob hwanchigi*)
- Drug trafficking (under Narcotics Control Act)
- Tax evasion (where threshold met)
- Bribery and political-funding offences
- Stock-price manipulation and other capital-market crimes
- Gambling-proceeds layering
- Romance scams (*lo-mansu sukaem*) and BCM-investment-scam variants
- Trafficking in persons
- Cybercrime and unauthorised electronic-financial-service access
- Terrorism financing
- North Korea sanctions evasion (parallel obligations under domestic sanctions law)

### Tipping-off (비밀 유지 의무 *bigi yuji eumu*)
FTRA Article 12 prohibits the *Bogo Gigwan* and its officers and employees from disclosing the fact of an STR to the customer or to any unauthorised party. Customer-facing communications about transaction holds, EDD requests, or account closures must comply.

### Penalties for non-filing
FTRA Article 17 prescribes administrative fines up to KRW 30 million and Article 18 prescribes criminal penalties (imprisonment up to 1 year and/or fines up to KRW 10 million) for officers found responsible for non-filing or for tipping-off. The 2021 amendments substantially raised penalties for VASPs.

### Practical tips
- KoFIU publishes quarterly *Sangye Sarye Mun-hak* (suspicious-pattern case examples) — incorporate the reference number where the situation matches.
- For VASP filings, KoFIU's 2021-onwards guidance series lays out the FTRA Article 5-2 expectations on travel-rule data exchange and high-risk wallet screening.
- For DPRK / North Korea-nexus cases, expect parallel interest from the Ministry of Foreign Affairs (sanctions desk) and the National Intelligence Service. Coordinate disclosures via KoFIU rather than directly to law-enforcement agencies.
- For voice-phishing-victim mule cases, the FSC's investor-protection framework allows for rapid account-freeze under the Special Act on Voice Phishing — but this is independent of the STR obligation and should not be conflated.

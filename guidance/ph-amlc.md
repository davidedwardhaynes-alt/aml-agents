## Philippines — STR Filing Guidance

### Legal basis
- **AMLA** (Anti-Money Laundering Act of 2001, Republic Act No. 9160, as amended by RA 9194, RA 10167, RA 10365, RA 10927, RA 11521)
- **AMLA-IRR** (Implementing Rules and Regulations)
- **TFPSA** (Terrorism Financing Prevention and Suppression Act of 2012, RA 10168)
- **ATA** (Anti-Terrorism Act of 2020, RA 11479)
- Sectoral rules: **BSP Circular 1022** (banks AML/CTF); **BSP MORB / MORNBFI Part 9**; **BSP Circular 1108** series (EMIs / virtual asset service providers); **SEC AML Rules** (capital markets); **IC Circular Letter 2018-60** (insurance); **AMLC Resolution** series (casinos and DNFBPs); **Casino IRR**

### Who must file
**BSP-supervised:** universal banks, commercial banks (KBs / UKBs), thrift banks, rural banks, cooperative banks, non-stock savings and loan associations (NSSLAs), quasi-banks, money service businesses (MSBs / RTCs), electronic money issuers (EMIs), virtual-asset service providers (VASPs), pawnshops, foreign exchange dealers, and offshore banking units.

**SEC-supervised:** broker-dealers, investment houses, mutual funds, financing companies, lending companies, pre-need companies (transferred to IC), securities clearing and settlement institutions.

**IC-supervised:** life insurance companies, non-life insurance companies, mutual benefit associations, HMOs, pre-need companies.

**AMLC-direct:** casinos (POGO and integrated resorts under PAGCOR / CEZA / APECO), DNFBPs (jewellery / precious metals / stones dealers, real-estate brokers and developers, lawyers and accountants where they prepare or carry out specified transactions on behalf of clients).

### Threshold for filing
"Knows or has **reasonable ground to suspect**" that a transaction:
- has no underlying legal or trade obligation, purpose or economic justification;
- the client is not properly identified;
- the amount involved is not commensurate with the client's business or financial capacity;
- the transaction does not match the client's known business or transaction pattern;
- the transaction is structured to avoid being reported (smurfing);
- there is a deviation from a client's profile and past pattern; or
- any other circumstance suggests a predicate offence under AMLA s.3(i).

> **Reasonable ground** is an objective standard, not certainty.

### Timing
- File **within 5 working days** from the determination of suspicion (AMLA-IRR Rule 9.1).
- **Terrorism financing: file immediately** (TFPSA s.10, RA 10168) — and freeze the funds for 20 days under AMLC freeze authority.
- Covered Transaction Reports (CTRs) for single-day cash ≥ **PHP 500,000** are filed separately, also within 5 working days. CTR and STR are separate obligations — file both where both apply.

### Filing channel
**AMLC Portal** (the *integrated reporting system* run by AMLC, accessed through the Reporting Institutions' designated officers).

- Authentication: digital certificates issued by AMLC to designated AML compliance officers.
- Electronic XML / portal upload only; paper acceptance only by exception.
- Reporting institutions must register with AMLC and maintain a current list of authorised reporters.

### Required content
1. Identity of the subject and connected parties (incl. beneficial owners under the AMLA *True Identity* requirement).
2. Account / relationship details — including BSP-supervised institution branch and AMLC institution code.
3. Transactions giving rise to suspicion — date, channel (cash over-the-counter, InstaPay / PESONet, foreign correspondent, GCash / Maya wallet, crypto on-ramp / off-ramp), amount and PHP equivalent.
4. Reasons for suspicion — referencing AMLA s.3(i) predicate offence(s) and AMLC red-flag indicators.
5. Action taken by the reporting institution (frozen / monitored / closed) and tipping-off compliance.

### Predicate offences (AMLA s.3(i), abbreviated)
- Plunder, graft and corrupt practices
- Robbery, extortion, qualified theft
- Drug trafficking (Comprehensive Dangerous Drugs Act)
- Swindling / estafa, fraud
- Smuggling
- Violations of the Customs and Tariff Code
- Tax evasion (where threshold met)
- Hijacking, destructive arson, murder
- Terrorism and terrorism financing (TFPSA / ATA)
- Trafficking in persons
- Bribery, malversation of public funds
- Forgery and counterfeiting
- Pre-need, securities, banking, insurance and financing-company crimes
- Environmental crimes (illegal logging, illegal fishing, illegal mining)

### Tipping-off
AMLA s.9(c)(2) prohibits any director, officer or employee of a covered institution from communicating, directly or indirectly, in any manner or by any means, to any person the fact that an STR has been or is about to be filed. Customer communications about transaction holds, EDD or account closures must be drafted to comply.

### Penalties for non-filing or late filing
AMLA Section 14 prescribes administrative and criminal penalties for failure to file CTRs / STRs, including fines per violation per day and imprisonment for officers found responsible.

### Practical tips
- AMLC publishes annual typology bulletins and Resolution series — incorporate the Resolution number where the case fits (e.g. AMLC Resolution on BCM investment scams; Resolution on POGO mule recruitment).
- For OFW remittance corridor cases, cross-reference BSP Circular 1108 and TIN / NIK identifier matching across remittance partners.
- Casino STRs: cite the relevant Casino IRR section and link the suspicious play to chip-walking / cross-property redemption / junket-promoter typologies where the pattern fits.

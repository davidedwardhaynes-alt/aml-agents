# TrustSphere
## Daily AML intelligence for APAC compliance teams

**For MLROs and heads of financial-crime compliance at FIED-supervised institutions with regional exposure.**

---

### The problem

A senior MLRO at a Malaysian universal bank reads 5–10 regulator notices, 3–4 industry briefings, and a thematic-review letter or two in any given week. By the time the morning stand-up starts, half of it is stale, the FIED-specific implications haven't been mapped, and the cross-border view (Hong Kong, Singapore, Thailand) is fragmented across three vendor briefings — each priced for the global treasury team, not the financial-crime function.

### What TrustSphere delivers

Three things in the first hour of every business day:

**Five-minute audio briefing.** Two-host fireside conversation on what's moving across 10 APAC jurisdictions. Listen on the commute, before stand-up, or at desk. Mirrors the depth of an analyst-team rundown without the analyst team.

**Email digest at 07:00 MYT.** Top stories, FIED filings due in the next 90 days, horizon-scanning items (FATF mutual evaluations, BNM consultation closures, AUSTRAC Tranche-2 commencement timing). Scannable in 90 seconds, deep-readable in five minutes.

**STR rubric and obligation register.** A working tool, not a feed. The AMLA s.14 narrative drafting assistant tags every sentence as Analyst-Stated [A] or Inferred [I], matched to FIED's expected structure. Obligation register is statute-anchored with priority, entities-impacted, and penalty context per item.

All source material is publicly-available regulator notices and curated industry news. Where the specific regulator reference is verified against the live source, we say so. Where it isn't, we say that too. A regulatory-accuracy spot-check accompanies the product.

### Why now

Three pressures making FIED-supervised institutions need this in 2026:

- **Cross-border STR scrutiny.** BNM's revised circular on cross-border crypto exposure requires a positional baseline by 31 July. FIED inspections in H2 will test rollup quality.
- **AUSTRAC Tranche-2 spillover.** Malaysian banks with Australian branch operations face DNFBP guidance overlay starting 2026; AUSTRAC has been signalling early.
- **FATF mutual-evaluation cycle.** Singapore in 2027, Malaysia following. The twelve-month run-up is where institutions evidence the work.

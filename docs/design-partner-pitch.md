# Design Partner Pitch — the ask script

Use this when an MLRO has shown genuine interest in the live demo and you need to convert that interest into a committed pilot.

---

## The 60-second ask script

> "OK so you've seen what we have. Where we are: we have working v0 across SG / HK / MY / AU, validated against the actual filing requirements at each FIU, with sanctions screening, document analysis, and the consortium intelligence layer.
>
> What we don't have yet is depth in *your* institution's filing standard. Every bank has its own house style, MLRO preferences, and tolerance for assertive language. We need 3–5 design partners over the next 90 days to refine the product against real institutional standards.
>
> Specifically what I'd ask of you:
>
> 1. **One MLRO or senior analyst from your team**, two hours per month, walking us through 5–10 anonymised STRs and telling us what's wrong with our output.
> 2. **Permission to use your firm's name** in our materials post-pilot, only with your sign-off on language.
> 3. **Honest critique** — including 'this isn't useful, here's why'. We'd rather hear it now.
>
> What we offer in return:
>
> 1. **Free pilot for 60 days** — synthetic data only initially, your-cloud-region production option after.
> 2. **Direct line to me and the product team** — Slack channel, dedicated support.
> 3. **Custom rubric tuning** to your firm's standard — your STRs read like *yours*, not generic.
> 4. **50% off year-1 pricing** if you convert to paid post-pilot.
> 5. **First-mover positioning** as part of our launch consortium.
>
> Can we get 30 minutes on the calendar next week to scope?"

## Negotiation flexibility

These are the levers you can move:

**Open to flex on**:
- Number of seats (3–10 reasonable)
- Length of pilot (30–90 days)
- Specific integration scope
- Joint-marketing language

**Hold the line on**:
- Synthetic data first (Phase 1) — protects everyone
- Anonymised feedback notes (we have to learn from the pilot)
- Two hours / month — anything less and we can't materially improve the product

---

## Red flags — when to walk

Don't sign a design partner who:

- Won't commit to the 2 hrs/month feedback (signals they won't actually use it)
- Insists on production data on day 1 (signals their procurement is going to drag this out 6 months)
- Asks for exclusivity (we need 3–5 partners across different segments — exclusivity blocks our learning)
- Refuses to be named publicly post-pilot (signals they're not committed to outcome)
- Wants to negotiate price before they've used the product

A bad design partner is worse than no design partner. Walk if any of the above hits.

---

## Filling 3–5 slots

By segment, the ideal mix:

1. **One Tier-2/3 SG bank** (e.g. RHB SG, BPI SG, OCBC NISP)
2. **One HK virtual bank or VASP** (e.g. ZA Bank, Mox, HashKey)
3. **One Malaysian digital bank** (Boost, AEON, GXBank — your event-week meetings)
4. **One Australian DCE or Tranche 2 entity** (e.g. Independent Reserve, a mid-tier accounting firm)
5. **One DNFBP wildcard** (a SG real estate agency, HK TCSP, or AU law firm)

Why this mix: covers 4 jurisdictions × different entity types → fastest learning curve for the product.

# Follow-up Email Templates

Send within 24 hours of any meeting where the prospect showed any interest. Templates below — adjust for tone match with each prospect.

---

## Within-24-hours follow-up (always send)

**Subject**: Following up — AML Agents demo + your team's STR workflow

**Body**:

> Hi [First name],
>
> Great meeting you yesterday at the [SG Fraud & Financial Crime event / on the conference floor / over coffee]. Thanks for taking the time to walk through the AML Agents demo.
>
> Quick recap of what we discussed:
> - You currently file [N] STRs / month with STRO / JFIU / FIED / AUSTRAC
> - Your biggest workflow pain is [specific pain they mentioned]
> - You use [TM platform] and [sanctions vendor] today
> - You wanted to see [specific feature or output we discussed]
>
> Three things attached / linked:
>
> 1. **[Demo URL or recording]** — link to a hosted version you can play with directly. Login: demo / demo123.
> 2. **One-page summary** — attached PDF, share with your team.
> 3. **Design partner term sheet** — attached if you're considering a 60-day pilot.
>
> Next step: I'd like to get 30 minutes with your MLRO and 1–2 senior analysts next week — would Tuesday or Thursday afternoon work? [Calendly link]
>
> If a pilot isn't right timing, I'd still love to keep you in our advisory loop — we send a monthly product note covering APAC AML/RegTech updates, no sales pressure.
>
> Thanks again,
> [Your name]
> [Title]  ·  TrustSphere Partners (HK)
> [phone] · [email] · [LinkedIn]

## 1-week nudge (if no reply to first email)

**Subject**: Re: AML Agents demo — quick check-in

**Body**:

> Hi [First name],
>
> Following up on my note from last week — wanted to check in before this rolls into next month's priorities.
>
> Two quick options:
>
> 1. **You've considered and it's not the right fit right now** — totally understand. If you'd let me know, I'll close the loop and stop the follow-ups.
>
> 2. **You haven't had time to look properly** — happy to send a 90-second demo video instead of a meeting if your calendar is full. [video link]
>
> Either way, no pressure. Just don't want to leave you hanging.
>
> [Your name]

This template is short on purpose. Don't keep selling. The "give them an off-ramp" framing actually gets higher reply rates than another sales pitch.

>

>

>
> First scheduled feedback meeting: end of Week 2 (Calendly link to follow).

> Welcome aboard.

> [Your name]

## After they say no

**Subject**: Thanks for the conversation

**Body**:

> Hi [First name],
>
> Got it — and totally fair. Thanks for taking the time to evaluate.
>
> Three small asks:
>
> 1. **Anything specific that drove the no?** Genuinely useful for us to hear, even one line.
> 2. **OK to keep you in our monthly product note loop?** (No sales — just APAC AML/RegTech updates. Easy unsubscribe.)
> 3. **Anyone in your network you'd point us at?** A short intro from you would mean a lot.
>
> Thanks again,
> [Your name]

A polite no is gold — they often refer you to peers, especially if you handle the no with grace.

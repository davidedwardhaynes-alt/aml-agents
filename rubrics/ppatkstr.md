# STR rubric — Indonesia (PPATK / Laporan Transaksi Keuangan Mencurigakan)

You are drafting a Suspicious Financial Transaction Report — *Laporan
Transaksi Keuangan Mencurigakan* (LTKM) — for filing with **Pusat
Pelaporan dan Analisis Transaksi Keuangan (PPATK)**, the Indonesian
Financial Intelligence Unit, under Law No. 8 of 2010 on Prevention and
Eradication of Money Laundering Crimes (UU TPPU 2010), Law No. 9 of 2013
on Prevention and Eradication of Terrorism Financing, and the
implementing PPATK Heads of Regulation series.

## Voice

- Authoritative, factual, regulator-defensible.
- May be drafted in English, Bahasa Indonesia, or bilingual; default to
  English with Bahasa Indonesia for required statutory references and
  PPATK-prescribed terms.
- Cite the predicate offence categories under UU TPPU 2010 Article 2
  (e.g. corruption, narcotics, terrorism, smuggling, banking crimes,
  capital markets crimes, customs crimes, taxation crimes,
  insurance crimes, employment of unauthorised foreign workers,
  human trafficking, illegal arms trade, illegal logging, illegal
  fishing, illegal mining).
- Where the reporting institution is OJK-supervised (commercial banks
  *Bank Umum*, rural banks *BPR / BPRS*, insurers, capital-market
  intermediaries, fintech P2P lenders, multifinance companies), cite
  the relevant OJK Regulation (e.g. POJK 12/POJK.01/2017 on AML/CFT
  for financial-services sector, as amended).
- Where the reporting institution is BI-supervised for payment
  systems (e-money issuers, payment-service providers), cite the
  relevant Peraturan Bank Indonesia (PBI) — e.g. PBI 23/6/PBI/2021 on
  Payment Service Providers.
- Where the reporting institution is a non-financial profession
  (notaries, advocates, accountants, real estate agents, dealers in
  precious metals or stones, automotive dealers), cite PPATK Head
  Regulations on the Profesi specific reporting regime.

## Required structure

1. **Reporting institution and reporter** — *Pihak Pelapor* identity:
   name, OJK / BI / PPATK supervisor licence number, sector
   classification (Bank Umum / BPR / IKNB / fintech / non-financial
   profession), MLRO equivalent (*pejabat penanggung jawab anti
   pencucian uang*), STR reference, date of filing.

2. **Subject** — *Pengguna Jasa* (customer). For natural persons, full
   name, NIK (Nomor Induk Kependudukan), date and place of birth,
   occupation, address. For juridical persons, registered name,
   NPWP (Nomor Pokok Wajib Pajak), TDP / NIB (Nomor Induk Berusaha)
   business identification, beneficial owners (BO), business address.
   **Do not fabricate identifiers.**

3. **Triggering activity** — the transactions that crystallised
   suspicion. Date, amount in Indonesian Rupiah (and original currency
   if different), counterparty, channel (cash over-the-counter, BI-FAST
   real-time wire, RTGS / kliring, mobile-banking, e-wallet OVO /
   GoPay / DANA / ShopeePay, foreign correspondent wire, crypto-asset
   on-ramp via Bappebti-licensed *Pedagang Aset Kripto*).

4. **Pattern and predicate offence reasoning** — why the activity is
   suspicious. Reference the UU TPPU 2010 Article 2 predicate offences
   most likely to apply. Use PPATK-published typologies where they
   match (BCM-investment-scam mule-victim layering, KKN /
   corruption-proceeds layering through business expense
   reimbursement, narcotics-proceeds layering through e-wallet rapid
   movement, illegal-mining proceeds via gold-trading TBML,
   human-trafficking-linked OFW remittance abuse, terrorism-financing
   small-amount layering).

5. **CDD / EDD findings** — what the institution did and what it
   found. Specifically address PBI / POJK expectations on EDD for
   PEPs (*Politically Exposed Persons*), foreign customers, and
   customers in high-risk business sectors. Distinguish between
   documented CDD and EDD requested but not produced.

6. **Red flags observed** — list discretely. Reference PPATK-published
   red-flag indicators (Pedoman) by document number where applicable.

7. **Disposition / recommendation** — LTKM filed to PPATK via GRIPS
   (Gathering Reports & Information Processing System); account action
   taken (frozen, monitored, customer relationship terminated subject
   to tipping-off restrictions); cross-references to any prior LTKM
   on the same subject; safe-harbour invocation under UU TPPU 2010
   Article 28 (immunity for good-faith reporting).

## Style notes

- Currency: Indonesian Rupiah (IDR, Rp). Use thousands separators
  (e.g. Rp 5.420.000.000,00 — note: Indonesian decimal/thousand
  conventions use period for thousands and comma for decimal,
  but in English-language STRs the comma-thousands convention is
  conventionally adopted: IDR 5,420,000,000.00). State the original
  currency where the transaction is in foreign currency, with the
  IDR equivalent at the Bank Indonesia *Jisdor* reference rate.
- Counterparty country names: prefer ISO-recognised forms.
- Dates: ISO-8601 (YYYY-MM-DD) in operational sections; longhand in
  the lead paragraph.
- First reference uses both Bahasa Indonesia and English short-forms
  where the term is statutorily defined (e.g. *Pengguna Jasa*
  (customer); *Pemilik Manfaat* (beneficial owner); *Politically
  Exposed Person*).

## Tipping-off

UU TPPU 2010 Article 12 prohibits disclosing the fact of an LTKM to
the subject or to any unauthorised party. Customer communications
about transaction holds, account closures, or EDD requests must be
drafted to comply. Where tipping-off considerations have shaped a
communication, state this explicitly in the narrative.

## Threshold and timing

- The threshold for LTKM is **knows or has reasonable suspicion**
  that a transaction involves proceeds of a predicate offence under
  UU TPPU 2010 Article 2 (UU TPPU 2010 Article 23(1)).
- File **within 3 working days** from the determination of suspicion
  (UU TPPU 2010 Article 23(2)).
- For terrorism-financing transactions, file **immediately** under
  Law No. 9 of 2013.
- LTKT (Cash Financial Transaction Report) covers cash transactions
  ≥ IDR 500,000,000 in a single working day; LTKL (International
  Funds Transfer Report) covers cross-border transfers ≥ IDR
  100,000,000. LTKM is filed in addition to LTKT / LTKL where there
  is also suspicion, not instead.

## What this rubric does NOT do

- It does not invent customer identifiers, transaction amounts, or
  facts not provided by the analyst.
- It does not speculate beyond what is documented in the analyst's
  case file.
- It does not assert criminal intent — it describes patterns
  consistent with the predicate offence and leaves the
  determination to PPATK and law enforcement.

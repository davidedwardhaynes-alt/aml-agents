# STR rubric — Philippines (AMLC)

You are drafting a Suspicious Transaction Report (STR) for filing with the
Anti-Money Laundering Council (AMLC), Republic of the Philippines, under
the Anti-Money Laundering Act of 2001 (Republic Act No. 9160) as amended,
its Implementing Rules and Regulations (the AMLA-IRR), and the AMLC
Registration and Reporting Guidelines.

## Voice

- Authoritative, factual, regulator-defensible.
- No marketing language, no hedging where facts are clear, no speculation
  beyond what the analyst has documented.
- Reference the specific predicate offence under AMLA s.3(i) where the
  pattern matches one (e.g. plunder, drug trafficking, qualified theft,
  swindling/estafa, fraud against the government, terrorism, terrorism
  financing).
- Where the suspicion connects to BSP-supervised financial institutions
  (UKBs, KBs, EMIs, OFs, NSSLAs), cite the relevant BSP Circular (e.g.
  BSP Circular 1022 on AML/CTF for banks; BSP Circular 1108 series on
  EMIs; BSP MORB / MORNBFI Part 9).
- Where the reporting institution is SEC-supervised (broker-dealers,
  investment houses, mutual funds), cite SEC AML Rules.
- Where the reporting institution is IC-supervised (insurers,
  pre-need companies, HMOs), cite IC Circulars on AML/CFT.
- Where the reporting institution is a casino (POGO / IRR) or DNFBP,
  cite the AMLC Resolution series and the relevant Casino Implementing
  Rules and Regulations (CIRR).

## Required structure

1. **Reporting institution and reporter** — bank / EMI / casino / DNFBP
   identity, supervisor (BSP / SEC / IC / AMLC for casinos and DNFBPs),
   STR reference, MLRO sign-off, date of filing.

2. **Subject** — natural or juridical person. For natural persons, full
   name, date of birth where known, TIN, occupation, residence. For
   juridical persons, registered name, SEC registration number,
   beneficial owner(s), business address. **Do not fabricate identifiers.**

3. **Triggering activity** — the transaction(s) that crystallised
   suspicion. Date, amount in Philippine Peso (and original currency if
   different), counterparty, channel (over-the-counter cash, InstaPay /
   PESONet wire, foreign correspondent wire, GCash / Maya wallet,
   physical bearer instruments, crypto on-ramp / off-ramp).

4. **Pattern and predicate offence reasoning** — why the activity is
   suspicious. Reference one or more of the AMLA s.3(i) predicate
   offences where the pattern fits, or describe the typology in
   AMLC-published terms (mule-victim layering, BCM-investment-scam
   layering, politically exposed person source-of-wealth gap, casino
   chip-walking, OFW remittance corridor abuse, e-wallet rapid
   in-and-out, etc.).

5. **CDD / EDD findings** — what the institution did and what it found.
   Distinguish documented CDD from EDD requested but not produced.
   Specifically address the BSP MORB Part 9 expectation that EDD is
   conducted on PEPs, foreign customers, and customers in higher-risk
   business sectors.

6. **Red flags observed** — list discretely. Reference AMLC's published
   red-flag indicators by Resolution number where applicable.

7. **Disposition / recommendation** — STR filed to AMLC; account
   action taken (frozen, monitored, account closure initiated subject
   to tipping-off restrictions); cross-references to any prior STRs
   on the same subject; safe-harbour invocation under AMLA s.9(c)
   (immunity from civil and criminal liability for good-faith filing).

## Style notes

- Currency: Philippine Peso (PHP, ₱). Use thousands separators (e.g.
  PHP 5,420,000.00). State the original currency where the transaction
  is in foreign currency, with the PHP equivalent at the prevailing
  Bangko Sentral reference rate.
- Counterparty country names: prefer ISO-recognised forms (e.g.
  Hong Kong SAR, People's Republic of China, United States of America).
- Dates: ISO-8601 (YYYY-MM-DD) in operational sections; longhand
  ("15 April 2026") in the lead paragraph.
- Use the full official institution names on first reference (e.g.
  "Anti-Money Laundering Council (AMLC)") then the abbreviation.

## Tipping-off

AMLA s.9(c)(2) prohibits disclosing the fact of an STR to the subject
or any person other than law-enforcement officials or AMLC. Customer
communications about transaction holds, account closures, or EDD
requests must be drafted to comply with the tipping-off prohibition.
Where tipping-off considerations have shaped a CDD or RM communication,
state this explicitly in the narrative.

## Threshold and timing

- The threshold is **suspicious or has reasonable ground to suspect**
  (AMLA s.3(b-1)). It is an objective standard, not certainty.
- File **within 5 working days** from the determination of suspicion
  (AMLA-IRR Rule 9.1).
- For predicate offences with terrorism financing dimension, file
  **immediately** under the Terrorism Financing Prevention and
  Suppression Act of 2012 (RA 10168) s.10.
- Covered Transaction Reports (CTRs) for cash transactions exceeding
  PHP 500,000 in a single banking day are filed separately within
  5 working days; an STR is filed in addition where there is suspicion,
  not instead of the CTR.

## What this rubric does NOT do

- It does not invent customer identifiers, transaction amounts, or
  facts not provided by the analyst.
- It does not speculate beyond what is documented in the analyst's
  case file.
- It does not assert criminal intent — it describes patterns
  consistent with the predicate offence and leaves the determination
  to AMLC and law enforcement.
